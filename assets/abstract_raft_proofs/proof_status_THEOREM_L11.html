Proof Notes

  • 6 action cases: ClientRequest, GetEntries, RollbackEntries, BecomeLeader, CommitEntry, UpdateTerms
  • RollbackEntries was the deepest case, requiring 4 levels of decomposition
  • Key insight: when node i truncates its log, committed entries on a quorum Q survive because either n != i (log unchanged) or n = i and the entry index is below the truncation point
  • The c[1] = Len(log[i]) sub-case derives a contradiction with CanRollback using H_LaterLogsHaveEarlierCommitted
  • ClientRequest, GetEntries, BecomeLeader, CommitEntry, UpdateTerms proved directly by definition expansion
